About Terengganu

Terengganu, located on the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia, is a state renowned for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and traditional charm. It boasts pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and idyllic islands like Redang, Perhentian, and Kapas, making it a haven for nature and beach lovers. The state is also famous for its traditional crafts, including batik and songket, as well as iconic landmarks such as the Crystal Mosque and Terengganu State Museum. With its vibrant local festivals, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality, Terengganu offers a captivating blend of tranquility and cultural richness.

Exciting Facts about Terengganu
Terengganu holds the distinction of being the earliest state in Malaysia to welcome Islam, about 700 years ago. The state boasts a brass industry that has been thriving for over 300 years.
Terengganu is home to Malaysia's longest coastline, stretching 244 kilometers from north to south. The Redang and Perhentian Islands are globally renowned as some of the best diving destinations.
Tasik Kenyir, the largest man-made lake in Malaysia, features 340 islands formed from submerged hilltops. Terengganu's boat-building expertise is world-famous, crafting wooden seafaring yachts without blueprints or nails.
Stone Age fossils in Terengganu reveal evidence of early human dwellings dating back 8,000 to 20,000 years. The state's beaches were recognized by Lonely Planet as the best place to relax under coconut trees, and CNN ranked them among the world's best beaches in 2013.
The Terengganu State Museum is the largest museum in Southeast Asia. The Setiu Wetlands showcase nine unique ecosystems, three river basins, and a 14-kilometer lagoon, making it one of Malaysia's most diverse wetlands.
The Chemerong Waterfall in Dungun district is the tallest waterfall in the country. Terengganu is the only state in Malaysia with a traditional gamelan ensemble.
It is Malaysia's largest producer of batik textiles. Terengganu's songket is considered the finest in the nation.
